{\large \textbf{Spectral Filtering for Vision-Language-Action Models}} \hfill \textbf{Team:} Shivam Kak, Ishan Saha, Connor Brown \\
\rule{\textwidth}{0.4pt}

% --- Abstract ---
\section*{Abstract}

Vision-language-action (VLA) models such as $\pi_{0.5}$ predict action chunks via iterative denoising, but the transformer action expert has no inductive bias for long-range temporal structure across the chunk. We integrate two sequence-modeling primitives into the $\pi_{0.5}$ action head: \textbf{(1)} Spectral Transfer Units (STUs), which convolve along the action horizon with eigenvectors of a fixed Hankel matrix, and \textbf{(2)} a vanilla diagonal SSM (\emph{Mamba-style}) baseline that uses HiPPO-initialized state-space dynamics. The diagonal-SSM comparison isolates whether the Hankel-specific structure of STUs contributes anything beyond generic linear recurrence. We fine-tune all variants from the released $\pi_{0.5}$ \emph{base} checkpoint (not the LIBERO-finetuned checkpoint) on LIBERO across $H \in \{5, 10, 20, 50\}$. As a residual post-transformer block, both STU-K4 and Mamba-K4 \emph{hurt} the converged baseline by 4--5\% at $H{=}10$, with the gap shrinking only to $\sim$2\% at $H{=}50$. We then redesign the integration along the lines of a parallel \texttt{stu-dreamer-jax} effort: STU operates on the noisy action chunk (LDS input) injected \emph{before} the transformer, with zero-initialised output. This \textbf{STU-v2} ties baseline at every horizon ($H \in \{10, 20, 50\}$, all within $\pm$0.6\%), confirming the structural fix. To test whether the small action expert simply lacks capacity to absorb a new branch, we run two additional placements with the same zero-init recipe: \textbf{STU-v3} (output-side smoothness prior on the predicted velocity $v_t$) and \textbf{STU-v4} (pre-input branch on the PaliGemma prefix, where the VLM has $\sim 25\times$ more parameters than the action expert). Both also tie baseline at every $K$, horizon, and warmup tested ($\le 1.2\%$ in either direction). A direct check of the spectral parameters shows $M_k^\pm$ remains near zero throughout fine-tuning; a warmup probe (1k-step warmup so $M_k^\pm$ grows $2.5\times$) modestly hurts performance at every placement ($+1.7$ to $+2.0\%$). Together these results point to a stronger conclusion than ``budget-bounded'': spectral filtering does not add a useful inductive bias for LIBERO action prediction at \emph{any} of the four placements tested, whether on the small action expert or the much larger VLM backbone.

% --- Background ---
\section*{Background}

\textbf{VLA Models and $\pi_{0.5}$.}
The $\pi_0$ family [1] combines a SigLIP vision encoder and PaliGemma language model with a lightweight action expert. In the flow-matching variant ($\pi_{0.5}$), an action chunk $a \in \mathbb{R}^{H \times D_a}$ is produced by integrating a learned velocity field $v_\theta(x_t, t)$ via $x_{t+\mathrm{d}t} = x_t + \mathrm{d}t \cdot v_\theta(x_t, t)$ from $x_0 \sim \mathcal{N}(0, I)$. The transformer action expert sees the full chunk in parallel, so causal attention is the only mechanism that mixes information across the $H$ time positions of the chunk.

\textbf{Spectral Transfer Units.}
STUs [2] convolve input sequences with spectral filters from the Hankel matrix $Z_{ij} = \frac{2}{(i+j)^3 - (i+j)}$. The top-$K$ eigenvectors $\phi_k$, scaled by the fourth root of their eigenvalues, serve as filters:
\vspace{-0.5em}
\begin{equation}
y = \textstyle\sum_{k=1}^{K} \left( (u * \phi_k^+) \cdot M_k^+ + (u * \phi_k^-) \cdot M_k^- \right)
\end{equation}
\vspace{-0.7em}

\noindent where $\phi_k^\pm$ split each filter into even/odd-symmetric components, $M_k^{\pm} \in \mathbb{R}^{D_{\text{in}} \times D_{\text{out}}}$ are learnable projections, and convolution uses FFT in $O(L \log L)$.

\textbf{Vanilla SSM baseline (Mamba-style).}
To isolate the contribution of the Hankel structure, we add a diagonal continuous-time SSM baseline $\dot{x} = A x + B u,\, y = C x$ with HiPPO-LegS initialization [3]. We discretize via the bilinear transform and convolve with the resulting impulse response over the same horizon $L = H$, again with FFT. The state dimension $N$ plays an analogous role to STU's $K$. This control verifies that any improvement (or lack thereof) is specific to the Hankel structure rather than to generic linear recurrence.

% --- Method ---
\section*{Method}

\textbf{Architecture.}
We insert a single residual sequence block immediately after the transformer action expert and before the action projection. Given hidden states $h \in \mathbb{R}^{B \times H \times W}$, we apply
\vspace{-0.4em}
\begin{equation}
h' = h + \mathrm{Block}\bigl(\mathrm{LayerNorm}(h)\bigr), \qquad a = W_{\mathrm{out}}\, h',
\end{equation}
\vspace{-0.7em}

\noindent where $\mathrm{Block} \in \{\mathrm{STU}_K, \mathrm{SSM}_N\}$ operates along the action-horizon axis. The residual connection lets the network fall back to baseline behavior while the freshly-initialized block parameters train, and the LayerNorm decouples block training from the scale of the pre-trained transformer outputs.

\textbf{Implementation.}
\texttt{PI0STUPytorch} and \texttt{PI0MambaPytorch} both extend \texttt{PI0Pytorch}, overriding \texttt{denoise\_step} to inject the residual block at the same location. STU spectral filters are precomputed once at init and registered as non-persistent buffers. Trainable parameters are $M_k^{\pm} \in \mathbb{R}^{K \times W \times W}$ for STU, and $A, B, C$ for the SSM. For the Gemma 300M action expert ($W{=}1024$), STU-K4 adds $\sim 8.4$M parameters (2.8\% overhead); the SSM baseline is matched parameter-for-parameter.

\textbf{Training.}
All runs fine-tune from the released $\pi_{0.5}$ \textbf{base} checkpoint (\texttt{gs://openpi-assets/checkpoints/pi05\_base})---not the already-converged LIBERO-finetuned checkpoint, so that any inductive bias from the spectral block is not masked by the reference's saturation. Block weights are added with \texttt{strict=False} loading and trained jointly with the rest of the model. We use AdamW at $5{\times}10^{-5}$, cosine decay with 1000-step warmup, batch size 8, gradient clipping at 1.0, bfloat16, and 5000 steps on a single H100 / RTX-PRO-6000. The 14 runs in this report total roughly 20 GPU-hours.

\subsection*{STU-v2: a dreamer-aligned redesign}
\label{sec:stuv2}

\textbf{What went wrong with the original integration.} Concurrent work in our group on STU + DreamerV3 (Connor Brown's thesis) reports a sharp asymmetry: applying STU as a \emph{post-hoc} residual on the GRU output chain collapses scores to the random-policy floor (29 vs.\ baseline 940 on \texttt{dmc\_walker\_walk}), whereas inserting STU \emph{inside} the recurrence as an additional input branch on the action history improves performance by 20--25\%. Our original VLA integration is structurally the same as their failed post-hoc design: STU receives post-attention hidden states, with randomly-initialised projections that fight the converged transformer from step 0.

\textbf{Redesign.} STU-v2 makes three changes, each a direct port of the dreamer recipe:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.5em, nosep]
    \item \textbf{Operate on the LDS \emph{input}, not its output.} The STU consumes the noisy action chunk $x_t \in \mathbb{R}^{B \times H \times D_a}$ (the natural input to the action-prediction LDS, per Theorem 3.1 of [2]) instead of the post-attention hidden states.
    \item \textbf{Inject before the transformer, not after.} The spectral context is added to the action token embeddings \emph{before} they enter the action expert---analogous to the 4th GRU input branch (\texttt{dynin3}) in \texttt{stu-dreamer-jax}---rather than refining a converged representation post-hoc.
    \item \textbf{Zero-initialise the output projection.} $M_k^\pm$ are initialised to zero, so at step 0 the network is bit-identical to the baseline; the spectral component grows only as gradients flow.
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Result.} STU-v2-K4 and STU-v2-K8 both recover baseline performance to within run-to-run noise ($-0.5\%$ and $-0.4\%$ respectively), and the H=10 loss curves are visually indistinguishable from the no-block baseline from step 0 through step 5000 (Fig.~\ref{fig:h10}, all coloured curves overlap in the zoomed view). This is exactly the behaviour zero-initialisation predicts: at step 0 the spectral component contributes nothing.

\textbf{Direct check on $M_k^\pm$ growth.} Loading the two final checkpoints and inspecting the spectral projections directly, the entries of $M_k^\pm$ remain tiny: $\max|M^\pm_k| \approx 8 \times 10^{-3}$, $\overline{|M^\pm_k|} \approx 7 \times 10^{-4}$, with Frobenius norms $0.29$--$0.49$ across the full $K \times D_a \times W$ tensor. The corresponding default random-init scale would be $(KD_a)^{-1/2} \approx 0.088$, so after 5{,}000 steps the optimizer has only grown the spectral projection to about \emph{1\% of a typical layer's magnitude}. This makes the budget hypothesis concrete: the M-matrix has measurably moved off zero, but not enough to materially alter the network's output. Closing the gap to a positive result would either require a much longer training schedule (closer to dreamer's 500k-step regime), shorter LR warmup so the M-matrices receive useful learning rate within the 5k-step budget, or training from scratch rather than fine-tuning a converged checkpoint.

\subsection*{Placement ablation: output-side (v3) and VLM-side (v4)}
\label{sec:placement}

\textbf{Motivation.} Even with the v2 fix, fine-tuning a freshly-added branch on the converged 300M-parameter action expert may lack the parameter budget to escape the zero attractor: the action expert is small, brittle, and already saturated on LIBERO. The PaliGemma backbone has $\sim 7.5\times$ more transformer parameters and far more redundancy, so a zero-init STU branch attached to its input may have more headroom. We therefore run two additional placements that share the v2 recipe (zero-init out-projection, residual added to a pre-existing signal) but at different sites in the network:

\textbf{STU-v3 (output-side, smoothness prior on velocity).} Operates directly on the final predicted velocity $v_t \in \mathbb{R}^{B \times H \times D_a}$, $D_a{=}32$, just before integration into the next $x_{t+\mathrm{d}t}$. The most natural location for a ``smoothness across the action horizon'' prior, since $v_t$ \emph{is} the temporal sequence we want to smooth. Parameter cost: $K \times D_a \times D_a = 4 \times 32 \times 32 \approx 0.07\mathrm{M}$, tiny.

\textbf{STU-v4 (PaliGemma prefix input).} Mirrors v2's architecture on the VLM side: an STU on the prefix embedding sequence (image+language tokens, $\sim 968$ positions for LIBERO with 3 SigLIP images), output added as a residual masked by \texttt{prefix\_pad\_masks} \emph{before} the VLM forward, with zero-init out-projection. Width $W{=}2048$ for PaliGemma, so STU-K4 adds $4 \times 2048 \times 2048 \approx 33.6\mathrm{M}$ parameters (1.4\% of the $\sim 2.3$B-parameter VLM).

\textbf{Result.} Both variants tie baseline at $H{=}10$: v3-K4 reaches $0.0297$ ($-0.6\%$), v4-K4 reaches $0.0296$ ($-1.2\%$), v4-K8 reaches $0.0296$ ($-1.0\%$). The H=20 v4 run also ties: $0.0275$ vs.\ baseline $0.0276$ ($-0.3\%$). Across all placements ($\{$action-expert input, output velocity, VLM prefix input$\}$), all $K$ tested ($\{4, 8\}$), and both horizons tested ($\{10, 20\}$), the redesigned STU recovers baseline to within $\pm 1.2\%$---never significantly above, never significantly below.

\textbf{Generic-SSM control on the VLM side did not run.} We attempted a Mamba-v4 control to mirror the action-expert-side comparison from Table~\ref{tab:h10}, but the FFT-based diagonal-SSM convolution kernel requires a 32 GB allocation on the 968-position prefix sequence and OOM'd at step 0 on a 95 GB H100 (the kernel array has shape $[2L \times W \times W]$, large for $W{=}2048$). The result of interest---whether \emph{any} zero-init pre-input recurrence helps on the VLM side---is partially answered by v4 itself: it ties baseline, so a generic-SSM control that also tied would not change the conclusion. We leave a chunked-FFT or selective-scan implementation to future work.

\textbf{Warmup probe across all three placements.}
The most direct test of whether the 5{,}000-step training budget bottlenecks the spectral component is to shorten the LR warmup so $M_k^\pm$ receives meaningful gradient signal earlier in training. We rerun baseline and the K=4 variant at \emph{each} of the three zero-init placements (v2, v3, v4) at $H{=}10$ with \texttt{warmup\_steps=1000} (vs.\ 10{,}000 in the main sweep), reaching peak LR by step 1k and giving the optimizer 4k post-warmup steps. For v2, final $M_k^\pm$ Frobenius norms are $\sim 2.5\times$ larger ($\|M^+\|_F = 0.92$ vs.\ $0.36$, $\max |M^+_k| = 0.019$ vs.\ $0.008$), confirming the spectral component receives substantially more learning signal in this regime.

The combined evidence---all three zero-init STU variants tie baseline whenever $M_k^\pm$ is forced small, and all three slightly hurt when $M_k^\pm$ is allowed to grow---supports a stronger conclusion than ``budget-bounded'': on LIBERO action prediction, spectral filtering does not appear to add a useful inductive bias \emph{regardless of placement} (action-expert input, output velocity, or VLM prefix input) and regardless of how aggressively the spectral parameters are trained. The original VLA hypothesis---that a Hankel-eigenbasis filter would unlock long-range temporal structure---is not supported in this regime.

% --- Discussion ---
\section*{Discussion}

\textbf{No-improvement result holds, strengthened across four placements.}
The midterm conclusion---that adding an STU residual to the $\pi_{0.5}$ action head does not reduce training MSE on LIBERO---survives every refinement we ran: training from \texttt{pi05\_base} rather than \texttt{pi05\_libero}, a parameter-matched vanilla SSM baseline, and a full horizon sweep. Post-hoc residual STU still trails the baseline at every horizon tested. With the dreamer-aligned redesign (zero-init pre-input), STU recovers baseline---but does not exceed it---at \emph{every placement we tried}: action-expert input (v2), output-side velocity (v3), and VLM prefix input (v4). The PaliGemma-side placement (v4) tests the alternative hypothesis that the small action expert may have lacked headroom, since the VLM has $\sim 25\times$ more parameters; even there, no measurable gain.

\textbf{Hankel structure provides no measurable advantage over a generic SSM.}
At $H{=}10$, STU-K4 and Mamba-K4 land within their own per-run noise of each other (0.0316 vs.\ 0.0311), and the same is true at $H \in \{5, 20, 50\}$. So the gap from baseline is not due to anything specific to Hankel filtering; it is a property of inserting any newly-initialized linear recurrence into a converged transformer head. The Hankel structure is not the mechanism doing useful work in this setting.

\textbf{The horizon hypothesis is partially supported.}
At our midterm we conjectured that $H{=}10$ was simply too short for spectral filtering to help. The horizon sweep confirms the directional claim: the gap to baseline decreases monotonically from $+5.7\%$ at $H{=}10$ to $+1.9\%$ at $H{=}50$ for post-hoc STU-K4, and analogously for Mamba-K4. However, the gap does not close---if anything, the trend suggests the crossover horizon is well beyond what LIBERO action chunks support. Linear extrapolation of the four points crosses zero only around $H \approx 80$--$100$, a regime that LIBERO does not provide and that may exceed the practical control-frequency horizons any current real-robot setup uses. Importantly, the redesigned variants (v2, v4) already tie baseline at $H{=}10$ without any horizon increase: in their case the question of whether more horizon would help is moot, because the spectral component has not yet moved off zero.

\textbf{Limitations and what remains untested.}
We measure only training MSE; success rates on the 130 LIBERO tasks could differ even at near-equal MSE if STU produces smoother trajectories. The Mamba-v4 generic-SSM control on the VLM side did not run because the FFT kernel for a 968-position sequence at $W{=}2048$ exceeds H100 memory; a chunked-FFT or selective-scan re-implementation would close this gap. We also did not run STU-v3 at $H \in \{20, 50\}$ or STU-v4 at $H{=}50$ due to compute budget. Finally, the residual connection plus LayerNorm makes it easy for the optimizer to drive each STU block toward zero. The 1k-warmup probe partially addresses this---it forces the spectral parameters to grow $2.5\times$, and they then \emph{hurt} performance---so a positive result is unlikely to be hiding behind this self-attenuation, but a learned residual gate could verify directly.

% --- Conclusion ---
\section*{Conclusion}

We integrated four placements of spectral filtering into the $\pi_{0.5}$ stack and ran 26 LIBERO fine-tunes total: (i) a residual post-transformer STU at $K \in \{4,8\}$ and $H \in \{5,10,20,50\}$, (ii) a parameter-matched residual Mamba at the same configurations, (iii) a dreamer-aligned STU-v2 (pre-input on the action expert, zero-init) at $K \in \{4, 8\}$, $H \in \{10, 20, 50\}$, (iv) STU-v3 (output-side smoothness on velocity, zero-init) at $K{=}4$, and (v) STU-v4 (pre-input on the PaliGemma prefix, zero-init) at $K \in \{4, 8\}$ and $H \in \{10, 20\}$, each with a 1k-warmup probe. The post-hoc residual variants trail baseline by 2--6\% across all horizons, with the gap shrinking but not closing as $H$ grows; STU and Mamba track each other to within own-noise, so the Hankel structure provides no advantage over a generic linear recurrence here. The three zero-init redesigns (v2, v3, v4) all tie baseline at every $K$, every horizon, and every warmup setting tested ($\le 1.2\%$ in either direction)---confirming the structural fix from \texttt{stu-dreamer-jax}, and also confirming that the no-improvement result is not an artifact of the small action-expert: the VLM-side variant on a $25\times$-larger backbone behaves identically. Direct inspection of $M_k^\pm$ and the 1k-warmup probe across all three placements show that even when the spectral component is given $2.5\times$ more learning signal it consistently \emph{worsens} loss by 1.5--2.0\%, suggesting that, on LIBERO action prediction, spectral filtering simply does not add a useful inductive bias \emph{at any of the four placements tested}. The companion world-model report and the sister \texttt{stu-dreamer-jax} thesis explore the regime where spectral filtering does provide a measurable benefit: long autoregressive rollouts where small per-step errors compound, in which dreamer-style integration of STU on the action history reaches \texttt{dmc\_walker\_walk} score 920 in $\sim$320k steps versus baseline's $\sim$400k.
